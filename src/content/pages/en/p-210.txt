         Placement of final consonants

         The final consonant is positioned below the initial and
         medial sounds to complete the syllable. In the word 군 Gun,
         ㄴ is placed below 구 Gu. Similarly, in     [ŋəp], the
         consonant ㅂ is positioned below 어 Eo.


         Combination of identical letters

         The same letters can be combined and written side by side
         to create different meanings. In native Korean, 혀 Hyeo
         refers to the tongue inside the mouth, while   [xjə] means
         "to pull." Similarly, 괴여 Gwoeyeo means "I love someone,"
         whereas 괴     Gwoeyyeo means "I am loved by someone."
         Likewise, 소다 Soda means "to overturn and spill
         something," whereas 쏘다 SSoda means "to shoot
         something.”
