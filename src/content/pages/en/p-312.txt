    Historical background and the need for restoration

    The current structure of Hangul was established during the
    Japanese occupation, even before the discovery of the
    Hunminjeongeum Haeryebon. Restoring Jeongeum is not
    only important for modern technology and artificial
    intelligence but also essential for establishing cultural identity
    during a time of national division.
    Jeongeum Hangul could also be used to preserve endangered
    minority languages, extending the spirit of Hunminjeongeum
    to support linguistic diversity globally. Considering the rapid
    changes of the last century and preparing for the future, it is
    time to reconstruct modern Hangul based on the strengths of
    Jeongeum.
    This initiative requires not only collaboration between North
    and South Korea but also the involvement of global experts
    to optimize Hunminjeongeum for modern times and
    humanity at large.
