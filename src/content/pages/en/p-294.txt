In the full set of combinable Hangul Jamo characters in
    Unicode, there are 125 initial sounds, 94 medial vowels, and
    138 final sounds. Each set includes an empty element. When
    calculated by total code capacity, 125 × 95 × 138 results in
    1,638,750 possible sound combinations. However, this
    number includes cases where the initial or medial sounds do
    not actually exist.
    When forming a complete Hangul syllable, the final sound
    can be omitted, but the initial and medial sounds cannot.
    Therefore, excluding those two cases, the actual number of
    valid syllables is 124 × 94 × 138, which equals 1,608,528.
