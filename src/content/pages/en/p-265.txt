3. Obsolete tones in sound

    In modern Hangul, the concept of tone (pitch accent) is no
    longer used. Therefore, the examples of Hangul presented in
    the Hunminjeongeum Haeryebon also omit tone markings.
    Although tone has disappeared in modern Hangul, there are
    still homophones whose meanings change depending on the
    length of the sound. For example, the word 굴 gul, when
    pronounced with a long sound, means "cave," but when
    pronounced with a short sound, it means "oyster." Similarly,
    밤 bam, when pronounced with a long sound, refers to
    "night (time)," while with a short sound, it means "chestnut
    (nut)." Since the length of the sound is not marked in the
    writing, the meaning must be inferred from the context and
    situation.
