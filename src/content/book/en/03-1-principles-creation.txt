    Strengthening by adding strokes

    In Hangul, strokes are added to consonant letters to
    indicate a stronger sound.
    ㅋ (Keu-iK or Keu)[kʰ] is made by adding a stroke to ㄱ
    (Geu-iG) because it is pronounced with more force.


    The same principle applies to:
    ㄴ (Neu-iN) becoming ㄷ(Deu-iD), and
    ㄷ (Deu-iD) becoming ㅌ(Teu-iT).
    ㅁ (Meu-iM) becoming ㅂ(Beu-iB), and
    ㅂ (Beu-iB) becoming ㅍ(Peu-iP).
    ㅅ (Seu-iS) becoming ㅈ (Jeu-iJ), and
    ㅈ (Jeu-iJ) becoming ㅊ (Cheu-iCh).
    ㅇ [◌eu-i] becoming ㆆ [ʔeu-iʔ], and
    ㆆ [ʔeu-iʔ] becoming ㅎ (Heu-iH).
    In all these cases, the added strokes indicate a stronger or
    more emphatic sound.

         The throat is located at the back, and the molar sounds are

    Clarity and turbidity

    The clarity and turbidity of sounds can be categorized as
    follows:
    ㄱ ㄷ ㅂ ㅈ ㅅ ㆆ (G D B J S [ʔ]) are clear and pure plain
    sounds.
    ㅋ ㅌ ㅍ ㅊ ㅎ (K T P Ch H) are slightly stronger
    aspirated sounds, which are less clear than the plain
    sounds.
    ㄲ ㄸ ㅃ ㅉ ㅆ ㆅ (KK TT PP JJ SS HH[x]) are very turbid
    and heavy tense sounds.
    ㆁ ㄴ ㅁ ㅇ ㄹ ㅿ ([ŋ] N M ◌ R Z) are neutral resonant
    sounds, which are neither clear nor turbid.

    Neutral resonant sounds as the root consonants
    The basic principle of creating Hangul consonants is to use
    the neutral resonant sounds as the root shapes.
    ㄴ ㅁ ㅇ (N M [ɦ/◌]) are soft and gentle sounds that are not
    strong or sharp.
    In terms of clarity and turbidity, they come later in the order
    because they are softer.
    However, they are used as the root shapes for creating
    other consonants because of their neutral and stable form.
    ㅅ (S) and ㅈ (J) are very clear sounds, but ㅅ is less harsh
    than
    88   ㅈ. Therefore, ㅅ is used as a base shape for creating


￼
    other consonants.
    ㆁ[ŋ] is a velar nasal sound where the back of the tongue
    blocks the throat and the sound is released through the
    nose. Its sound is similar to ㅇ, which is why in ancient
    phonology, ㆁ and ㅇ were often interchanged.

    Although ㆁ is shaped after the throat, it is not used as the
    base shape for creating molar(velar) sounds.
    Throat sounds correspond to Water in Daum, while molar
    sounds correspond to Wood. Even though ㆁ is a molar
    sound, its sound is soft and moist, resembling a sprout
    emerging from water.
    ㄱ represents a tree rmly rooted in the ground, ㅋ
    symbolizes a tree growing lushly, and ㄲ represents an old,
    strong tree. Thus, ㄱ ㅋ ㄲ(G K KK)[k k͈ kʰ] all re ect the
    characteristics of Wood.
    This is why ㄱ (G) is the base shape for creating molar
    sounds, even though it is associated with the throat.
    The root shapes for consonants are ㄱ ㄴ ㅁ ㅅ ㅇ(G N M

    These shapes were chosen because they are neutral and
    stable, serving as the foundation for creating all other
    consonants.

    Clear to turbid sounds

    When the very clear sounds ㄱ ㄷ ㅂ ㅈ ㅅ(G D B J S) are
    written side by side, they become the very turbid sounds ㄲ
    ㄸ ㅃ ㅉ ㅆ (KK TT PP JJ SS). This is because very clear
    sounds become heavy and turbid when they slow down and
    thicken.

    However, for the throat sounds, instead of using a very
    clear sound, the aspirated clear sound ㅎ(H) is used to
    create the very turbid sound ㆅ(HH)[x]. This is because:
    ㆆ ([ʔ]: Yeorin Heuih) is a deep sound that does not thicken,
    so it cannot become turbid. ㅎ(H), on the other hand, is
    shallower than ㆆ, so it thickens and becomes a very turbid
    sound (tense sound).

    Therefore, the very turbid sounds are ㄲ ㄸ ㅃ ㅉ ㅆ ㆅ(KK
    TT PP JJ SS [x]), totaling six characters.

         Medial vowels, the cosmic harmony


         There are 11 medial vowel letters in total. They are based
         on the principles of Hattangsa(Heaven ㆍ [ʌ], Earth ㅡ (eu)
         [ɯ], and Human ㅣ(i) re ecting the cosmic harmony of the
         universe.

         Heaven, the origin of sounds
         ㆍ (Hanul-A [ʌ]) represents Heaven. It is pronounced with
         the tongue slightly curled, creating a deep and resonant
         sound. This sound is profound and far-reaching,
         symbolizing the vastness and depth of the universe.
         The circular shape of ㆍ represents the roundness of
         Heaven, re ecting the ancient belief that Heaven is round
         and encompassing. The sound produced is deep and
         primordial, symbolizing the origin of all sounds.
         According to the principles of BalkIEo, ㆍ symbolizes the
         beginning of all things, just as midnight marks the end of
         one day and the start of a new one. It represents the
         moment when Heaven opens and life begins. In this sense,
         ㆍ is the most fundamental vowel in Hangul. It symbolizes
         the origin of sound and the beginning of life, just as Heaven
         is the source of all existence.

    Medial vowels vary in depth, being pronounced with either

    darkness and light, forming the energy of Daum and is the
    work of Heaven.
    Initial consonants are either empty or full, rising or
    stagnating, and are pronounced heavily or lightly. They
    include empty throat sounds, cold molar sounds, ying
    tongue sounds, obstructed tooth sounds, and heavy or light
    lip sounds.
    Through these qualities, initial sounds express strength and
    softness, achieving harmony in Daum and can thus be seen
    as the work of Earth.

    Balk(darkness and light), become deeper or shallower, with
    sound produced by rounding or opening the mouth.
