          4. Explanation of the final sounds


          Final sounds completing syllables

          The nal sound follows the initial sound through the medial
          sound, completing the character’s sound and syllable.

          For example, in the word 즉 JeuK, the nal sound is ㄱ(iK),
          a molar sound, which is placed at the end of 즈 Jeu to form
          즉 JeuK.
          Similarly, in      HHoNG [xoŋ], the nal sound is ㆁ ([ŋ], nasal
          sound), which is placed at the end of         [xo] to complete the
          syllable       [xoŋ].
          This principle applies to all tongue sounds, lip sounds ,
          teeth sounds , and throat sounds.


          Tone and strength

          Sounds are distinguished by their speed and clarity:
          Soft and slow tones form level tones, rising tones, and
          departing tones. Short and fast tones form checked tones.
          Letters with neutral resonance (unclear or resonant sounds)
          do not produce strong sounds. Therefore, when used as
               nal sounds, they are suitable for level, rising, and
          departing tones.

         However, the eight nal sounds ㄱ ㆁ ㄷ ㄴ ㅂ ㅁ ㅅ ㄹ (익

         Therefore, in nal positions, ㅈ (iJ) and ㅊ (iCh) are often
         replaced by ㅅ (iT) for simpli cation. Additionally, the ㅇ
         sound is clear and hollow, so it is not used as a nal
         consonant1. When ㅇ appears in the initial position, it is
         silent [◌], allowing the medial vowels alone to form a
         complete syllable.

    Checked tone to softer nal sounds

    The semi-tongue sound ㄹ (R/L) is used in native Korean
    words but is not found in Hanja. For example, the Hanja
    character with the sound of 볃 ByeoT preserves the
    checked tone with the nal sound ㄷ(iD). However, over
    time, people habitually softened the nal sound ㄷ in Hanja
    pronunciation, replacing it with the lighter ㄹ sound, leading
    to 별 ByeoL.
    As a result, while 볃 ByeoT retains its checked tone, 별
    ByeoL has lost this quality, as its sound spreads and slows
    down, preventing it from functioning as an entering-tone
    syllable.

                  A resonant sound that is neither clear nor turbid can be
                  used as a nal sound in level tone, rising tone, and
                  departing tone. But it cannot form an checked tone.
                  Very clear sounds, next clear sounds, and very murky
                  sounds all form checked tones, making their pronunciation
                  very fast.

                  It is natural for an initial sound to also be used as a nal
                  sound. However, using only eight characters ㄱ ㆁ ㄷ ㄴ ㅂ
                  ㅁ ㅅ ㄹ (익                    읻 인 입 임 잇 일) (iK iŋ iD iN iB iM iT iL) is
                  suf cient to express all nal sounds.
                  Even in places where ㅇ could be used as a nal sound, it is
                  omitted, and the medial sound alone is enough to complete
                  the syllable.
