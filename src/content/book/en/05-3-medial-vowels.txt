     3. Explanation of medial sounds


     The medial sound is placed at the center of the character's
     sound, connecting the initial and nal sounds to complete
     the syllable. They serve as the bridge that uni es the
     beginning and end of a sound, ensuring the harmony and
           ow of pronunciation.

     The medial sound of the word            [tʰʌn] is ㆍ[ʌ]. Since ㆍ is
     positioned between ㅌ(T) and ㄴ(N), it forms the sound
     [tʰʌn].
     The medial sound of the word 즉 Jeuk is ㅡ(eu)[ɯ]. Since
     ㅡ is positioned between ㅈ(J) andㄱ(G), it forms the sound
     즉 Jeuk.
     The medial sound of the word 침 Chim isㅣ(i). Since ㅣ is
     positioned between ㅊ and ㅁ, it forms the sound 침 Chim.
     In the same way, the medial sounds ㅗ ㅏ ㅜ ㅓ ㅛ ㅑ ㅠ
     ㅕ (o a u eo yo ya yu yeo) complete the sounds in words
     like       [xoŋ], 땀 TTam, 군 Gun,        [ŋəp], 욕 Yok,     Zyang,
     슏 Syut, and 볃 Byeot by positioning themselves between
     the initial and nal sounds to form complete syllables.

    When two characters combine to form a single medial
    sound, they follow natural principles of formation.
    ㅗ (o) and ㅏ (a) both originate from ㆍ, so when combined,
    they form ㅘ (oa)[wa].
    ㅛ (yo) and ㅑ (ya) both originate from ㅣ(i), so when
    combined, they form ㆇ (yoya)[joja].
    ㅜ (u) and ㅓ (eo) both originate from ㅡ(eu), so when
    combined, they form ㅝ (ueo)[wə].
    ㅠ (yu) and ㅕ (yeo) both originate from ㅣ, so when
    combined, they form ㆊ (yuyeo)[jujə].
    Since these sounds come from the same source and
    belong to the same group, their combination remains in
    perfect harmony without any discord.

    When a single medial sound (basic vowel) ㆍ ㅡ ㅗ ㅏ ㅜ
    ㅓ ㅛ ㅑ ㅠ ㅕ ([ʌ] eu o a u eo yo ya yu yeo) combines with

    ㅒ ㆌ ㅖ (eo eui oi ai ui Yol yai yui yeoi) [ʌ ɯj oj aj uj əj
    joj jaj juj jəj].

    When two medial sounds combine with ㅣ, it forms 4
    additional sounds: ㅙ ㅞ ㆈ ㆋ (oai ueoi yoyai yuyeoi) [waj
    wəj jajaj jujəj]

    The reason ㅣ can harmonize with both deep and shallow
    sounds, as well as open and closed sounds, is that it allows
    the tongue to extend and the mouth to open more naturally,
    making pronunciation easier.
    Furthermore, this re ects how humans participate in
    understanding and expressing all things in the world,
    demonstrating their ability to connect and communicate
    universally.
